## Academic & Cognitive Science Research

- **Adamo et al.**: Satisfaction of search review (2021)
  - Cited for: Satisfaction-of-search errors account for up to one-third of radiological reading errors

- **Berbaum et al.**: Satisfaction of search in diagnostic radiology, Investigative Radiology (1990)
  - Cited for: Finding one lesion produces statistically significant reductions in perceptual accuracy for additional lesions

- **Berthet**: Overconfidence across professional domains, Frontiers in Psychology (2022)
  - Cited for: Overconfidence is the most recurrent bias across management, finance, medicine, and law

- **Blair & Maron**: Legal document retrieval study (1985)
  - Cited for: Attorneys believed they retrieved 75% of relevant documents but actually found ~20% — 55pp calibration gap

- **Braun**: Time pressure and auditor judgment (2000)
  - Cited for: Time-pressured auditors maintained quantitative accuracy but filtered out qualitative fraud indicators

- **Carlile**: Knowledge boundaries framework, Organization Science (2004)
  - Cited for: Syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic barriers at professional boundaries; different processes needed to overcome each

- **Cassell, Dearden, Rosser & Shipman**: Archival evidence of confirmation bias in auditing (2022)
  - Cited for: Confirmation bias survives audit quality control processes; auditors with low-risk clients fail to respond to risk increases

- **Cowan**: Working memory capacity revision (2001)
  - Cited for: Working memory approximately 4±1 items (revised from Miller's 7±2)

- **Croskerry**: Dual-process model of clinical reasoning (2009, 2013)
  - Cited for: Type 1 processing dominates ~95% of decisions; System 2 preferentially impaired under cognitive load

- **Donnelly et al.**: Simulated DD review study (2019)
  - Cited for: Nine lawyers reviewing 50 contracts agreed on location but not extent of relevant material; DD outcomes are partly a function of which lawyer reviews

- **Drew, Vo & Wolfe**: Gorilla in the lung, Psychological Science (2013)
  - Cited for: 83% of radiologists failed to see a gorilla image 48x larger than a lung nodule; eye-tracking confirmed most looked directly at it

- **Dror**: Expert bias, Analytical Chemistry (2020)
  - Cited for: Expertise may increase certain biases; experts engage in more selective attention; "expert immunity" is a fallacy

- **Durkin & Rose**: Time pressure and confirmation of client assertions (2025)
  - Cited for: Time pressure promotes confirmation of management's assertions; auditors less likely to attend to disconfirming evidence

- **Endsley**: Situation awareness model, Human Factors (1995)
  - Cited for: Attention and working memory as critical bottleneck factors; ~18% of situation awareness errors from mental model problems

- **Evans et al.**: Low-prevalence miss rates in screening (2013)
  - Cited for: Low-prevalence conditions produce 2.5x higher miss rates

- **Fortune/Baruch Lev & Feng Gu**: Analysis of 40,000 acquisitions over 40 years (November 2024) - https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/we-analyzed-40000-mergers-acquisitions-ma-deals-over-40-years-why-70-75-percent-fail-leadership-finance/
  - Cited for: 70-75% of acquisitions fail to create shareholder value

- **Galbraith**: Information processing theory, Interfaces (1974)
  - Cited for: Self-contained task units reduce processing demands but sacrifice integration; no lateral relations across DD workstreams

- **Gompers, Gornall, Kaplan & Strebulaev**: How Do Venture Capitalists Make Decisions?, Journal of Financial Economics (January 2020) - https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2019/08/20/how-do-venture-capitalists-make-decisions/
  - Cited for: VCs spend average 18 hours/week with portfolio companies; 55-hour workweeks

- **Graber, Franklin & Gordon**: Diagnostic error in internal medicine (2005)
  - Cited for: Cognitive factors contributed to 74% of errors; premature closure is the single most common cause

- **Grossman & Cormack**: TAR vs exhaustive manual review, TREC Legal Track (2011)
  - Cited for: Technology-assisted review achieved results superior to exhaustive manual review

- **Hahn, Lawson & Young**: Information Load and Decision Quality, Psychology & Marketing (1992)
  - Cited for: Inverted U-shaped relationship between information load and decision quality under time pressure

- **Hansen**: Collaboration barriers research
  - Cited for: Not-invented-here, hoarding, search, and transfer barriers; "collection of silos" as cardinal sin

- **Heuer**: Psychology of Intelligence Analysis, CIA (1999)
  - Cited for: Additional information increases confidence without proportionally improving accuracy; dangerous gap between perceived and actual quality

- **Iselin**: Information load and decision performance (1989)
  - Cited for: Decision performance falls when information cues exceed ~10 items

- **Klein**: Recognition-Primed Decision model (1993)
  - Cited for: Experienced professionals recognize and act rather than analytically comparing; dangerous in genuinely novel situations

- **Krupat et al.**: Premature closure across experience levels (2017)
  - Cited for: No significant differences in diagnostic accuracy across students, residents, and faculty; experience did not protect

- **Lawrence & Lorsch**: Organization and Environment (1967)
  - Cited for: Highly differentiated firms more likely to fail without adequate integration

- **Perrow**: Normal Accidents theory (1984)
  - Cited for: In interactively complex, tightly coupled systems, catastrophic failures are inevitable

- **Reason**: Swiss Cheese Model of organizational accidents
  - Cited for: Catastrophe occurs when weaknesses across multiple organizational layers simultaneously align

- **Roitblat, Kershaw & Oot**: Document review agreement study (2010)
  - Cited for: Two independent teams agreed only 28% of the time; original review cost $14M over 100-hour weeks; automated systems outperformed both teams

- **Shanteau**: Expert competence and domain characteristics, Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes (1992)
  - Cited for: Expertise fails in domains with unpredictable outcomes, delayed feedback, and unique stimuli — matching DD characteristics

- **Simon**: Satisficing model (1955)
  - Cited for: Under constraints, decision-makers adopt aspiration-level thresholds and stop at the first satisfactory option

- **Simons et al.**: Inattentional blindness synthesis (2024)
  - Cited for: Under high-load conditions, only 8% noticed unexpected objects vs 47% under low load — sixfold decrease

- **Starmer et al.**: I-PASS structured handoff program, NEJM
  - Cited for: Reduced medical errors 23% and preventable adverse events 30% across 9 hospitals; 47% reduction in broader 32-hospital study

- **Vaughan**: Normalization of deviance concept
  - Cited for: Unsafe practices become culturally accepted because they haven't yet produced visible catastrophe

- **Voorhees**: Expert assessor agreement, NIST data (2000)
  - Cited for: Mean overlap of 32.8% on relevance judgments; practical upper bound ~65% precision at 65% recall

- **Wangerin**: Due diligence quality and acquisition outcomes, Contemporary Accounting Research (2019)
  - Cited for: Less DD associated with lower profitability, higher goodwill impairments; competitive pressures and agency problems reduce DD

- **Wickens**: Multiple Resource Theory / attentional tunneling (2008, 2009)
  - Cited for: Maximum interference when tasks compete for same processing resources; 17/24 pilots failed to detect critical events under high workload

- **Wohlstetter**: Pearl Harbor: Warning and Decision, RAND (1962)
  - Cited for: Attack presaged by signals buried in irrelevant noise; foundational signal-noise framework

- **Zegart**: Spying Blind — CIA, FBI, and the Origins of 9/11 (2007)
  - Cited for: 23 missed opportunities due to structural problems, cultural pathologies, and perverse incentive systems

- **Marriott/Starwood** ($13.6B, 2016): Cybersecurity breach since 2014 missed during DD; 500M guest records; £18.4M ICO fine; $1B+ estimated losses

- **Bayer/Monsanto** ($63B, 2018): DD limited to law firm memoranda; IARC classification + MDL discovery documents missed; $10B+ in settlements across 67,000+ lawsuits

- **BofA/Countrywide** ($2.5B, 2008): 60-person DD team over 90 days missed shadow guidelines; $60B+ in post-close losses

- **HP/Autonomy** ($11.1B, 2011): 7 advisory firms; revenue inflated 12-15% annually; $8.8B writedown; financial DD was ~6 hours of conference calls

- **Kraft Heinz** (2015 merger): $15.4B impairment; $208M fictitious cost savings; SEC investigation; declining brands + aggressive purchase price allocation

- **Caterpillar/Siwei** ($654M, 2012): Fraud discovered post-close when physical inventory reconciled against accounting records; $580M writedown

- **DaimlerChrysler** ($36B, 1998): Cultural incompatibility never assessed in DD; sold Chrysler in 2007 for ~$7.4B

- **Steward Health Care/Cerberus**: $9.2B in liabilities; largest hospital bankruptcy in US history; "immediate jeopardy" violations 32 times 2019-2024; monitoring ended, crisis followed

- **Envision Healthcare/KKR** ($9.9B, 2018): EBITDA collapsed from $1B+ to $250M; No Surprises Act; $3.5B equity wiped out

- **H.I.G. Capital/South Bay Mental Health Center**: $25M False Claims settlement despite majority board control
